What is Pediatric Physiotherapy?

Pediatric physiotherapy is a specialized branch of physical therapy that focuses on helping children improve their movement, strength, balance, and overall physical function. Our licensed pediatric physiotherapists work with children who have developmental delays, neurological conditions, or physical impairments to maximize their mobility and independence.

💡 The Power of Movement: Physical activity isn't just about exercise—it's crucial for brain development, learning, social interaction, and building confidence. Early physiotherapy intervention can significantly impact a child's long-term development and quality of life.

We use evidence-based therapeutic exercises, specialized equipment, and play-based activities to help children achieve their physical milestones, reduce pain, improve posture, and develop the strength and coordination needed for daily activities.

Signs Your Child May Need Physiotherapy

👶

Delayed Motor Milestones

Late to roll, sit, crawl, stand, or walk compared to typical development

⚖️

Balance & Coordination Issues

Frequent falls, clumsiness, or difficulty with stairs and uneven surfaces

🦴

Muscle Weakness

Poor muscle tone, difficulty supporting weight, or tires easily

🚶

Abnormal Gait Pattern

Walking on tiptoes, limping, or unusual walking patterns

😣

Pain or Stiffness

Complaints of muscle or joint pain, limited range of motion

📐

Postural Problems

Poor posture when sitting or standing, slouching, or asymmetry

🏃

Difficulty with Sports/Play

Struggles with running, jumping, catching, or physical activities

🧘

Poor Endurance

Tires quickly during physical activities or avoids active play
