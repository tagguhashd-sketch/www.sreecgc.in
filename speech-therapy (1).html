What is Speech Therapy?

Speech therapy is a specialized healthcare service that helps children overcome communication difficulties and develop clear, effective speech. Our certified speech-language pathologists work with children who experience challenges in speaking, understanding language, or expressing themselves.

💡 Did You Know? Early intervention in speech therapy can significantly improve a child's communication skills, academic performance, and social interactions. The earlier therapy begins, the better the outcomes!

Speech therapy addresses various aspects of communication including articulation (pronouncing sounds correctly), language comprehension, expressive language, voice quality, fluency (stuttering), and social communication skills.

Common Signs Your Child May Need Speech Therapy

👶

Delayed Speech Development

Not speaking or using fewer words than peers of the same age

🔤

Articulation Difficulties

Difficulty pronouncing certain sounds or words clearly

😰

Stuttering or Stammering

Repeating sounds, syllables, or words; prolonging sounds

❓

Limited Vocabulary

Using fewer words or struggling to learn new words

🤔

Difficulty Understanding

Trouble following directions or understanding questions

😶

Trouble Expressing Thoughts

Difficulty forming sentences or organizing thoughts into words

🗨️

Voice Quality Issues

Hoarse, nasal, or very soft/loud voice

👥

Social Communication Challenges

Difficulty with conversations, taking turns, or reading social cues
