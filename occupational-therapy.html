What is Occupational Therapy?

Occupational Therapy (OT) is a specialized healthcare service that helps children develop the skills they need for daily activities and independence. Our certified occupational therapists focus on improving fine motor skills, sensory processing, self-care abilities, and overall functional independence.

💡 Did You Know? Occupational therapy doesn't mean job training for children! "Occupation" refers to any meaningful activity a child does, including playing, learning, eating, dressing, and interacting with others.

OT helps children master essential life skills such as writing, buttoning clothes, using utensils, playing with toys, and participating in school activities. It also addresses sensory processing issues that can affect a child's ability to function in everyday situations.

Signs Your Child May Need Occupational Therapy

✍️

Poor Fine Motor Skills

Difficulty with writing, cutting, buttoning, or tying shoelaces

🤸

Gross Motor Delays

Trouble with balance, coordination, running, or jumping

👕

Self-Care Struggles

Difficulty dressing, feeding, or grooming independently

🎨

Sensory Sensitivities

Over/under-reaction to sounds, textures, lights, or smells

😣

Low Attention Span

Difficulty focusing on tasks or following instructions

👁️

Visual-Motor Issues

Trouble with hand-eye coordination or copying shapes

🎯

Poor Spatial Awareness

Frequently bumping into things or misjudging distances

😰

Behavioral Challenges

Meltdowns in response to sensory input or routine changes
